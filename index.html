About Me

My name is Cayla Dedrick. My pronouns are she/her or they/them. I'm a first year astronomy grad student at Penn State, working with Jason Wright. I'm also involved in the MINiature Exoplanet Radial Velocity Array (MINERVA) Collaboration. My research is focused on the detection and characterization of exoplanets, and getting a finicky set of telescopes to work the way we want them to (i.e. software development and day-to-day debugging).
I graduated in 2020 from Caltech, where I majored in Astrophysics, with a minor in Geological & Planetary Sciences. During undergrad, I worked with Heather Knutson and BJ Fulton on model-based detection of planet-like signals in radial velocity data. You can read about our project below. In the summer 2019, I was participated in the CASSI Program at Carnegie Observatories, where I worked with Josh Simon and Johanna Teske on searching for planets around metal poor stars with TESS.

When I'm not working, I like rock climbing, skiing, taking the utmost advantage of my NYT Crossword Subscription, and getting yelled at by my cat. I also enjoy singing and practicing my new ukulele skills. Sometimes I make pretty art.
